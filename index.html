  • Governing Equations of fluid flow

  • Laminar and turbulent flow
Governing Equations of Fluid Flow These equations represent
  • Conservation of mass
  • Conservation of momentum (Newton's second law of motion)
Also conservation of mass and momentum themselves are not sufficient to solve fluid problems, and we need subsidiary relations to solve these problems. So we use Stokes relation between stress and rate of strain. Notable point is that when we talk about Stokes equation, we restrict ourselves to the Netwonian fluid. The resulting equations are known as Navier-Stokes equations. These consist of
  • An equation for conservation of mass, called the equation of continuity
  • Equation for conservation of each component of momentum
